# Adversarial Test - BASB and PARA

## Why this exists

A system can sound internally coherent and still be weak.
This test pushes the vault against BASB and PARA, not because they are enemies, but because they are strong reference points.

## What BASB/PARA do well

- broad simplicity
- quick action-oriented organization
- easy explanation
- low barrier to initial understanding

## Where BASB/PARA would likely critique this vault

### 1. It is more complex
True.
This vault is more complex because it is trying to model:
- roles
- masks
- access
- truth layers
- quote provenance
- contradiction
- AI retrieval

### 2. It risks over-systematizing identity
Also true.
If used badly, this system could become an elaborate museum of the self.

### 3. It may slow capture
Also true if templates become mandatory too early in moments that should stay light.

## Where this vault critiques BASB/PARA

### 1. BASB/PARA are too flat for layered self work
They handle information well.
They do not natively handle:
- hidden layers
- role leakage
- mask management
- quote provenance
- raw versus approved output

### 2. BASB/PARA are weaker for AI-ready self-modeling
PARA organizes material.
It does not by itself build a durable profile stack or retrieval pods.

### 3. BASB/PARA assume action categories are primary
For this system, action categories are important but not sufficient.
The user also wants:
- recorded rational self
- mode-aware output
- layered truth
- reasoning continuity

## Verdict

Use PARA as floor and retrieval spine.
Do not let PARA define the whole system.

## Required guardrails from this test

- keep projects easy to find
- do not make every note identity-heavy
- let raw capture stay light in the inbox or daily notes
- reserve heavy metadata for durable notes
- review complexity every month
